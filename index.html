Anime

Anime usually comes in three formats:
TV series: Each episode is about 24 minutes long, there are 12-13 episodes per quarter, and each series must span an integer number of quarters. Anything longer than 2 quarters is considered "long running" or "mainstream crap".
OVAs: are released directly on cassettes, floppy discs or other carrier media that are in vogue at the time. Since they do not have to worry about TV censorship, many OVAs are unusually sexy or gory.
Especially many TV series have OVAs that consist mainly of fanservice, and all hentai are released as OVAs. OVAs are usually 1-6 episodes long, with each episode being about 30 minutes long.
Movies 1-2 hours long, like a live action movie.
Compared to TV, anime movies usually look prettier and are more action-packed.
Hayao Miyazaki is famous for his anime films like Spirited Away, and recently Makoto Shinkai has also found success with anime films like Your Name.
A sufficiently popular anime can encompass all three formats, with a couple of seasons of the series TV, a fanservice OVA and a film as the grand finale.
In terms of content, "anime" is about as meaningless as "TV " - it can be anything from situation comedy to crime thriller to action.
